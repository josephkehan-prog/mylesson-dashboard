While students may show how they arrived to \ntheir final answer, only the final answer will be scored.\n2-Credit Constructed-Response Questions\nThe 2-credit constructed-response questions require students to complete a task and show their work or \nexplain their answer. Many of the 2-credit constructed\u0002response questions will assess conceptual application and understanding.\n3-Credit Constructed-Response Question\nThe 3-credit constructed-response question asks students to show their work or explain their answer in \ncompleting two or more tasks or a more extensive problem. The 3-credit constructed-response question \nallows students to show their understanding of mathematical procedures, conceptual understanding, and \napplication. The 3-credit constructed-response question may also assess student reasoning and the ability to \ncritique the arguments of others. \nAdditional Assessment Resources\nThe New York State Question

Technology knowledge and skills are vital for full participation in 21st \nCentury life, work, and citizenship. \nThe New York State K12 Computer Science and Digital Fluency Learning Standards were adopted by the \nBoard of Regents in December 2020. The new standards focus on fve key concepts: Impacts of Computing, \nComputational Thinking, Networks and Systems Design, Cybersecurity, and Digital Literacy. Each concept \ncontains two or more sub-concepts, and within the sub-concepts are a number of standards. The standards \nare grouped into grade-bands: K-1, 2-3, 4-6, 7-8, and 9-12. \nThe related areas of computer science and digital fuency have been combined under one “umbrella” to create \na comprehensive, cohesive set of learning standards that represents the essential knowledge and skills in \nthese areas that all students should possess upon graduation in order to be successful in college, careers, and \ncitizenship in the 21st century, with a vision that every student will know how to live productively and safely in a \ntechnology-dominated world. This includes understanding the essential features of digital technologies, why \nand how they work, and how to communicate and create using those technologies. \nAs with all NYS Learning Standards, the Computer Science and Digital Fluency Standards should be \nimplemented with careful understanding of child development and developmentally appropriate practice, \nespecially for our youngest learners. The academic foundation that is set in the early years is essential, and the \nsocial emotional needs and environment for learning are key ingredients for student success. As these standards \nare implemented, it is important to meet the needs of the “whole child,” recognizing that a well-rounded \neducation, positive learning environment, strong home-school connection, and high expectations all contribute \nto student success. \nThe need to promote computer science and digital fuency education among all students comes at a time \nwhen the system is already charged with building up language skills among an increasingly diverse student \npopulation. It is imperative that teachers promote inclusion and advance equity using technology and build \nstudents’ digital fuency while being culturally and linguistically responsive. In addition to instruction that is \ndevelopmentally appropriate, multilingual learners and English Language Learners beneft from activities, \nresources, and scafolds in the home language to learn essential knowledge and develop fuency in digital \ntechnologies.
